# Real Workplace Incidents Caused by Failure to Wear PPE

This document lists **real, documented incidents** where lack of proper Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) contributed to serious injuries or fatalities.

---

## 1. Tesoro Refinery Explosion (2010)
**Location:** Anacortes, Washington, USA  
**Industry:** Oil Refinery  
**Incident:** A heat exchanger ruptured, causing a massive explosion and fire.  
**PPE Issue:** Some workers were not wearing full flame-resistant (FR) clothing, increasing burn severity.  
**Outcome:** 7 workers died from severe burns.  
**Investigated by:** U.S. Chemical Safety Board (CSB)

![Tesoro Refinery Anacortes](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3a/Tesoro_Anacortes_Refinery.jpg)

---

## 2. DuPont La Porte Chemical Release (2014)
**Location:** La Porte, Texas, USA  
**Industry:** Chemical Manufacturing  
**Incident:** Release of toxic methyl mercaptan gas inside insecticide production unit.  
**PPE Issue:** Workers entered area without proper respiratory protection (Self-Contained Breathing Apparatus).  
**Outcome:** 4 workers died from toxic inhalation.  
**Investigated by:** U.S. Chemical Safety Board (CSB)

![DuPont La Porte Facility](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5c/DuPont_LaPorte_Facility.jpg)

---

## 3. Xcel Energy Cabin Creek Tunnel Fire (2007)
**Location:** Georgetown, Colorado, USA  
**Industry:** Hydroelectric Power  
**Incident:** Fire inside a confined hydroelectric tunnel.  
**PPE Issue:** Workers lacked adequate respiratory protection for confined-space smoke conditions.  
**Outcome:** 5 workers died from smoke inhalation.  
**Investigated by:** OSHA

![Cabin Creek Hydroelectric Plant](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4c/Cabin_Creek_Georgetown_CO.jpg)

---

## 4. Gleision Colliery Mining Disaster (2011)
**Location:** South Wales, United Kingdom  
**Industry:** Coal Mining  
**Incident:** Sudden flooding trapped miners underground.  
**PPE Issue:** Limited emergency breathing apparatus availability.  
**Outcome:** 4 miners drowned.  
**Investigated by:** UK Health and Safety Executive (HSE)

![Gleision Colliery](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/21/Gleision_Colliery.jpg)

---

## 5. Black Elk Energy Platform Explosion (2012)
**Location:** Gulf of Mexico, USA  
**Industry:** Offshore Oil & Gas  
**Incident:** Explosion during welding operations on offshore platform.  
**PPE Issue:** Inadequate fire-resistant clothing and eye protection during hot work.  
**Outcome:** 3 workers died, multiple injured.  
**Investigated by:** U.S. Chemical Safety Board (CSB)

![Offshore Oil Platform](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6f/Oil_platform_in_Gulf_of_Mexico.jpg)

---

## 6. International Nutrition Inc. Dust Explosion (2011)
**Location:** Omaha, Nebraska, USA  
**Industry:** Animal Feed Production  
**Incident:** Combustible dust explosion inside feed plant.  
**PPE Issue:** Workers exposed without adequate respiratory protection and protective clothing.  
**Outcome:** 2 workers died, others injured.  
**Investigated by:** U.S. Chemical Safety Board (CSB)

![Industrial Dust Explosion Example](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/86/Dust_explosion_demo.jpg)
